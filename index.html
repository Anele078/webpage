Gqeberha

Gqeberha previously known as Port Elizabeth, or 'PE' as it is known locally, is a port city and popular holiday destination on South Africa's Eastern Cape Sunshine Coast, famed for its outstanding beaches, watersports, waterfront, animal attractions, and, above all, its friendly people. The picture on the left represents one of the famous places to visit in Gqebherha, Donkin Reserve, it is a small park founded in 1820 by Sir Rufane Donkin, the founder of Port Elizabeth. The heartbreaking pyramid-shaped memorial to Sir Donkin's wife, Elizabeth, is a feature of the park, with the words "To the remembrance of one of the most flawless of human beings who has given her name to the town below" etched on it. Union Buildings

The Union Buildings, designed and built by Herbert Baker and completed in 1913, hold the Union of South Africa's administrative offices. It is still the official seat of government and houses the President of South Africa's offices today.

National Zoological Garden of South Africa

The National Zoological Garden of South Africa is a zoo in Pretoria, South Africa, that spans 80 acres. It was founded by J. W. B. Gunning in 1899 and is South Africa's national zoo. Pretoria Zoo is one of the world's eight largest and most highly regarded zoos.

church square

Church Plaza, formerly Market Square, is a historic square in the heart of Pretoria, Gauteng, South Africa. Marthinus Pretorius, the city's founder, decided that the plaza would be utilized as a market and a churchyard.
